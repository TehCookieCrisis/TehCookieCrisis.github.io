Concept | A rebel who don't need no cause
Jack is an Iconoclast of Clan Brujah and a canon character of the World of Darkness franchise and Bloodlines in particular. He is an Anarch of some renown and battles authority and hierarchy just for the sake of it.

Name | Smiling Jack
The only indication we have in canon, for any "real" Name Jack might be bearing, is in London by Night, where he is listed as his childe Herbert Westin's sire "Smiling" Jack Drake. I sincerely doubt though, that it is his mortal name, as he refuses to speak much of the time prior to his embrace and it feels like a pseudonym - naming himself after a legendary privateer he must have heard stories about, when he was a boy (Sir Francis Drake).
However, in the old version of his character sheet, I put his birthname as William John Baldey.

Origin | British by birth, Caribbean by choice
The canon information doesn't give much more than Caribbean pirate, but not much otherwise. Since his birth name is unknown, we can only guess by the color of his skin and birthdate, that he is likely European.
The old character sheet I made him a native of Bristol, England. Reading up on the history, he was likely an early buccanier and had frequent violent run-ins with the Spanish.

Nature |
Bravo The Bravo is a tough and a bully, and often takes perverse pleasure in tormenting the weak. To the Bravo's mind, might makes right. Power is what matters, and only those with power should be respected. Naturally, physical power is the best kind, but any kind will do. The Bravo sees overt threats as a perfectly reasonable means of gaining cooperation. The Bravo is not incapable of pity or kindness; he just prefers to do things his way. Robbers, bigots, and thugs are all Bravo Archetypes. A Kindred Bravo may abuse authority the system has granted him, or he may — violently or otherwise — force other Kindred out of his way to get what he wants.He's almost certainly an asshole.

Demeanor |
Rebel The Rebel is a malcontent, never satisfied with the status quo or the system as it is. He hates the very idea of authority and does everything in his power to challenge and undermine it. Perhaps the Rebel truly believes in his ideals, but it is just as likely that he bears authority figures some ill will over a “wrong” done to him in the past. A Rebel may hate institutions categorically, or she may be consumed with opposition toward a single particular authority. Teenagers, insurrectionists, and nonconformists all exemplify the Rebel Archetype. Rebel Kindred certainly include the Sabbat, Anarchs, and temperamental Brujah, but also have room for Tremere who chafe at the rigid Clan hierarchy or neonates who deplore their sires.
— Regain a point of Willpower whenever your actions adversely affect your chosen opposition. Rebels may oppose the government, the Church, a vampire Prince, or any other holder of authority. The player should choose whom or what his character rebels against when he adopts this Archetype. Storytellers should take rebellion against “all authority” with a grain of salt, and apply the Willpower reward for such a broad concept to only greater and greater actions of insurgency.
(V20 Core p. 94)

Official from LA by Night.
As for choosing whomst to rebel against: it is mostly authority in general, with whom Jack has a problem, as this is a developmental issue from mortal days, but also a philosophy taken to the extreme. It's not necessarily, that he is against the very concept of hierarchy - a ship needs a captain to be steered - but he sees himself as the balancer to keep the level of hierarchy to a minimum. If there absolutely has to be a particular enemy to rebel against it would be the Ventrue and, to a lesser degree, the Spanish.

Childer | Dre, Shelly & more...
Both mentioned official from Lore of the Clans.
Jack is a menace to society. He doesn't see it that way, but that's how a lot of people would describe him. It is for a whole variety of reasons, however, one of them surely has got to be Jack's careless siring of Caitiff and orphaned fledgelings.
There is method to the madness, as ellusive as that method may be, but Jack tries to generally only sire those whomst he thinks have a fighting chance. Jack doesn't make Shovelheads, he wants to make Brujah. And Brujah are sure as fuck not made by handholding. And maybe he also wants scare those old fucks shitless with all the Caitiff suddenly running around like they are harbingers of Gehenna. And if Gehenna is real, well... Why wait?

Be that as it may, Jack usually likes to leave a fledgeling whenever he travels outside of LA. A little present for the local prince to deal with. Dre, a Chicagonian Neonate embraced 25 years ago, an Shelly, a new acquisition to the Clan within the same city, are but two of the many siblings throughout the continential United States, Canada and Mexico. There are a few outside of North America (if they are still kicking, that is), but, since Jack travels there more rarely, they aren't as numerous.

Contrary to popular belief, Herbert Westin is not actually Jack's progeny, even though he seems to tell everybody that. Some asshole calling himself "Smiling" Jack Drake must have turned that punk sometime in the 1850s, gave his brain a good washing and beat the damn whelp black and blue until his eventual emancipation. Not Jack's style. But everytime he's in New York someone gives him shit about Westin. You got the wrong Brujah, pal.

Owner of the Last Round
The Last Round is a dive bar in Downtown Los Angeles that the local Anarchs are known to frequent. The owner is human - someone has to pay the bills - but either straight up knows a lot of his clients are vampires or suspects but doesn't give a shit. Either way, he doesn't ask questions, keeps the booze coming, and lets Jack crash in the cellar.
Jack, in turn, is a loyal patron and occasionally assumes the role of both entertainer and bouncer. The former, due his natural charm, general disposition and the mortal remnants of his alcoholism; the latter due to him being particularly effective when it comes to dispelling bar fights or shoving an armed robber's gun up his ass.


A Curator at the Natural History Museum
We all need a hobby to occupy our nights. And when Jack's Humanity drops too low or he needs too... cool it with the explosives and violence for a little, he considers himself a bit of a patron of history scholars. As per usual, part of this past-time is self-serving in nature, but he considers it a win-win for all. After all, wouldn't we all want to know more about sleeping mummies in the Middle East?
Either way, he has a friend who is a curator at the NHM in South LA, a mousey mid-40-year-old named Mildred, who is a rather shy soul that likes to burry herself in her work and is happy if she can lore-dump to someone. She's ultimately just a sweet soul and Jack feels a little bad even, that he shamelessly used the information he got from her to intercept the ship bringing the sarcophagus from Ankara...

Languages |
English (native language) English is Jack's native language, so you'd think he didn't have to learn it. And while true, he didn't have to learn them the way he learned French or Spanish, he did have to make an effort to keep up. He is actually a native speaker of Early Modern English and is a contemporary of Shakespeare (by technicality, the poet died when Jack was 5). However, the Bristolian drawl of a 17th century sailor is not something that comes easy to him nowadays. As mentioned before, Jack is keeping up. Which means that seamless switching into a dialect that no one speaks with him anymore is not as automatic - like remembering how you used to talk when you were a teen and asking you to switch seamlessly.
Nowadays Jack sounds broadly American. No region accent, maybe a little bit Los Angeles flavored, but that's it.

French Jack was a buccanier - one of the very earliest. Back in those early colonial days you had to learn who your friends and foes were real fast. When Spain was still the major player in the Caribbean, it was the French and the English who had to stick together to stick it to the Dons in their Galleons full of gold and black powder.
Jack's French isn't fantastic, he frequently uses Creole terms and it's just as vulgar and dirty as you'd imagine a seaman's yappings to be. It sounds a tad Quebecois at times, mostly because the French he learned is just very old and French Creole, which he does speak fluently, is also based off of a much older version of the language. That being said, he understands it, but doesn't use it much. He does go to Montréal every now and then, though. Great Poutine, shit-ass politics.

Spanish As mentioned before, Jack was already in the Caribbean, when the Spanish crown still claimed all of the "New World" for itself, because their oh-so-great Admiral Colón discovered the Americas and now it belongs to them forever. Nope. Jack waged war as a naval soldier against these bastards until the Treaty of Madrid was signed in 1630. And then he turned pirate and continued fighting against them. So it was actually very useful, knowing what the hell they were writing in their correspondences or whatever they were shouting at you before you stab them in the eyesocket.
Turns out Spanish should become a mainstay in the Caribbean, though, even after the Spanish left for good. There are just a lot of Spanish speakers in the Caribbean and the United States, so keeping up with the most recent lingo was not actually that hard. And don't forget the true Marxist-Leninist Brujah revolution in the 1950s Cuba, which Jack spent entirely in Havana, regretting to this very day that he didn't embrace Che when he had the time. This left Jack with a Cuban accent nowadays, even though he doesn't sound like a native speaker - there's actually a hint of Creole in his speech.

Dutch Jack doesn't actually like the weird pseudo-English language from the small canal-nation from across the English channel, but these motherfuckers were prolific slave traders and you cannot live through the entire colonialization of the Caribbean, without encountering slavery for the majority of it. As young Jack grew into his philosophy on freedom, he began to think that it might be fun to sink Dutch slave ships and royally fuck the Dutch wherever he saw them. Yet again, it's a matter of simply understanding ship logs and shipmen begging for mercy.
His Dutch isn't great and he frequently uses English words for the ones he doesn't know in Dutch but its understandable and he can read it alright. It's relatively close to English, so Jack kinda learned via osmosis, never really bothering to actually sit down and learn it.

French Creole Jack is actually accentlessly fluent in Haitian Creole. It was the lingua franca you used in the West Indies. He knew it as Saint-Domingue Creole when he first picked it up, but it was what you should know if you wanted to speak with the local population and he's been speaking it for centuries at this point. When coming first to Los Angeles in the 1920s, he actually had a bit of a Caribbean accent speaking English, just because he had lived in the Caribbean for so long. Though he has been told he sounds a but like a boomer nowadays, thanks to having made the United States his main base of operations and not keeping up with how the kids talk as much.
It is always a great party trick, though, to go to a Haitian restaurant, and have this pasty biker-looking white boy order some Pate Kode in perfect kreyòl ayisyen.
